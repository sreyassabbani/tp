## Design Context

### Users
Teleparty Clone Lab is for people prototyping and judging realtime watch-party experiences. The primary user is someone hosting a room for friends or collaborators and deciding whether a frontend feels cinematic, coordinated, and alive under realtime conditions.

### Brand Personality
Warm, cinematic, intentional. The interface should feel like a screening room control surface rather than a generic dashboard. It should read as confident and tactile, not corporate or toy-like.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light-mode first, with editorial typography, warm paper-tinted surfaces, and control-room contrast where the stage needs depth. Motion should feel paced and deliberate, like cues in a screening session, not constant micro-animation. Avoid generic SaaS cards, neon dark-mode tropes, and default developer-tool styling.

### Design Principles
1. Treat the product like a live screening desk, not an admin console.
2. Use warm materiality and strong typography to keep the interface memorable.
3. Let motion clarify tempo and hierarchy instead of decorating everything.
4. Keep dense realtime controls readable through clear grouping and progressive disclosure.
5. Prefer distinctive, composable components over giant route files full of one-off markup.
